# Adam Robinson | Strategic B2B Framework

### Core Content Architecture

- Treats customer discovery as the first stage of product development. Before launching RB2B, Robinson conducted more than 300 discovery conversations to validate demand, refine positioning, and identify early adopters before building a scalable product.
- Uses a "sell before you build" methodology that prioritizes customer commitment over product development. Ideas are validated through conversations, pre-sales, beta commitments, or payment signals before meaningful engineering investment occurs.
- Builds founder-led content around public experimentation and operational transparency. Organic social is used to develop content-market fit, attract prospects, and generate discovery conversations rather than drive immediate conversions.
- Relies on highly differentiated positioning through simple, memorable messaging. RB2B's "person-level website visitor identity" positioning became both the product narrative and the primary demand-generation asset throughout launch.

### Operational Metrics & Tactics

- Structures validation around a four-step process: create a clear positioning statement, conduct discovery calls, secure beta users, and build only after meaningful demand signals appear.
- Uses manual onboarding instead of automation during early stages. RB2B customers were onboarded through direct interaction for months before launch, creating continuous feedback loops that accelerated product refinement.
- Prioritizes product development through a weighted evaluation framework that considers customer value, request frequency, and implementation effort rather than building features reactively.
- Operates with strict efficiency constraints during early growth. Robinson advocates delaying hires until product-market fit is established, minimizing software expenses, and keeping teams intentionally small through the first stages of scale.

### Distinctive Insight

- Most founders build products and then search for customers. Robinson reverses the sequence by finding customers first, validating demand through direct conversations, and using those insights to determine what should be built.
