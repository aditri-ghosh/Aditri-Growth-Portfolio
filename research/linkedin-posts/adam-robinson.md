# Adam Robinson | Strategic B2B Framework

### Core Content Architecture

- Robinson treats LinkedIn as the primary channel for pre-launch discovery—not a late-stage growth hack. For RB2B, he ran 300+ discovery calls between October 2023 and March 2024 using LinkedIn DMs to his network, second-degree intros, and paid-advisory outreach templates before the product even had a website.
- His content system is built on "work in public" and founder-led narrative, not feature announcements. He posts daily on organic social not to chase immediate reach, but to find voice and achieve content-market fit—then uses LinkedIn Thought Leader Ads to put spend behind posts the platform already signals as high-performing.
- The sell-before-you-buy framework drives all early messaging: a sizzling one-liner (RB2B's was "person-level website visitor identity"), a four-slide quick pitch under five minutes, and discovery calls structured around whether prospects will pay before anything is built—eliminating the "build first, find buyers later" failure mode he lived through at Robly.

### Operational Metrics & Tactics

- Inbound-led outbound is his four-step pipeline machine: (1) drive traffic via LinkedIn, podcasts, events, and partnerships; (2) identify person-level website visitors with RB2B; (3) retarget via email, LinkedIn, and phone workflows; (4) concentrate sales on bottom-of-funnel prospects only. He claims this yields ultra-low CAC, ~50% higher close rates, and two-thirds shorter sales cycles.
- He bootstrapped Retention.com from 1 to 10M ARR in 27 months with six people, then learned the inverse lesson when hiring 50 people in 60 days collapsed SDR productivity: five SDRs still booked one meeting per week each, proving SDRs capture demand rather than create it.
- Pre-1M ARR operating rules: no hires beyond co-founders, Google Sheets instead of CRM until 10M ARR, manual onboarding calls before any automation, and a feature-prioritization sheet weighted by requester value, frequency, and build time, reviewed every two weeks.

### Distinctive Insight

- Original demand creation (organic social, podcasts, events) cannot be measured like paid channels—but scaling what you can measure (SDRs, outbound volume) while under-investing in unmeasurable demand sources is the core reason pipeline breaks; the fix is deliberately scaling "directionally correct" demand activities even when attribution fails.
