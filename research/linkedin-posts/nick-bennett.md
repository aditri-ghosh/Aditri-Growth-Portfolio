Most B2B influencer programs fail before they start.

Not because creators don't work. Because teams pick the wrong ones.

Here's the framework I use to evaluate whether a B2B influencer is worth partnering with. It's in my book, but I'll give you the short version.

Audience fit over follower count.

10k followers in your ICP beats 100k followers who will never buy. Pull their comments, not their impressions. Are the people engaging actually in roles that matter to your pipeline?

Content alignment over content volume.

Posting every day means nothing if the topics don't ladder to what you sell. Look at their last 20 posts. Do they talk about problems your product solves? If not, you're paying for reach that doesn't convert.

Creator credibility over creator polish.

The best B2B creators aren't always the ones with the cleanest videos or the best hooks. They're the ones whose audience trusts their recommendations because they've done the work. Practitioners beat performers.

Collaboration history over promises.

Ask for examples of past brand work. How did they integrate the product? Did they just mention it once or build it into their content naturally? One-off shoutouts don't move pipeline.

That's the filter. Audience fit, content alignment, credibility, and collaboration history.

If you want the full framework with more ways to approach B2B influencer marketing today, it's all in the book.

B2B Influencer Marketing: Work With Creators to Generate Authentic and Effective Marketing.

## Post 2: Account-Based Marketing (ABM) - Converting Organic Signals into Pipeline

June 2026

Link: 

[https://www.linkedin.com/posts/nickbennett1_500-accounts-40k-spent-nearly-zero-pipeline-activity-7472643231323824128-SBWj?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAE8m65QBcqY4_RmKPosShZbFiMFHEs1jO2s](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/nickbennett1_500-accounts-40k-spent-nearly-zero-pipeline-activity-7472643231323824128-SBWj?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAE8m65QBcqY4_RmKPosShZbFiMFHEs1jO2s)

500 accounts. $40K spent. Nearly zero pipeline.

That was the ABM program I walked into two falls ago.

I asked how they picked the accounts. Answer: a firmographic filter in ZoomInfo. Industry, headcount, revenue range. That was it.

No intent signals. No engagement history. No input from sales on which accounts they actually had relationships with.

Just a spreadsheet that looked impressive in a board deck.

Six months later, they had 3 opps. Three.

We cut the list to 30 accounts. Every single one had at least two of these signals:

- Visited the website in the last 90 days
- Engaged with content on LinkedIn
- Had an existing relationship with someone on the sales team

Then we stopped running campaigns at them.

Personalized video from the AE. Not a templated sequence. An actual video referencing something specific about their business.

A case study from their vertical. Not the fluffy kind with stock photos and vague quotes. Real numbers, real problems, real outcomes, and real people.

An invite to a small dinner event. Eight people. Real conversation. Not a mass webinar where they're one of 200.

The AE videos got replies because they didn't feel like marketing. The dinners created conversations that turned into pipeline because there was nowhere to hide.

30 accounts. 11 moved to pipeline in one quarter.

Their previous 500-account list produced 3 opps in six months.

Most ABM programs fail because they start with a list instead of starting with a signal.

The technology works. The strategy behind it usually doesn't.
