I shared these 5 LinkedIn tips in 2022.

All 5 are still relevant. (especially number 3)

1. Write each post with a tangible outcome. A lot of posts here make you think, but then don't give you anything to "apply". Always make it applicable!

Fact: This increases Saves too!

1. My 1-1-1 rule: 1 post → 1 topic → 1 key message

Fact: This makes it easier to engage.

1. Never post and ghost. Leaving your posts unattended sends a signal to your audience that you simply do not care. "Oh but I don't have the ti.." Please. Stop.

Fact: You also build a community long term.

1. Use listicles and quotes whenever possible.

Fact: This text format is SEO- and LLM-friendly.

1. Repurpose your best comments into posts. I repurpose probably 80% of my comments. It's the easiest way to keep producing content long term.

Fact: This entire post is a repurposed comment. 😊

Anyhow...

The big message here is: Some LinkedIn best practices are overblown. Most of the same stuff still works. I'm telling you this for the 5th year in a row. Your call. ✌️

### Annotation

- Demonstrates practical frameworks for creating and repurposing LinkedIn content.

- Introduces the 1-1-1 rule to improve clarity and audience engagement.

- Highlights community-building, comment engagement, and content repurposing as sustainable growth mechanisms.

## Post 2: Velocity Mechanics for 2nd- and 3rd-Degree Organic Distribution

May 2026

Link: 

[https://www.linkedin.com/posts/alicjasmin_i-get-around-100000-new-followers-every-activity-7459912638773391361-73FN?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAE8m65QBcqY4_RmKPosShZbFiMFHEs1jO2s](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/alicjasmin_i-get-around-100000-new-followers-every-activity-7459912638773391361-73FN?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAE8m65QBcqY4_RmKPosShZbFiMFHEs1jO2s)

I get around 100,000 new followers every year.

So how can a LinkedIn beginner grow?

(also goes for experienced accounts)

1. Stay in your lane → I don't hop on trends. Ever. You'll never see a Claude prompt post from me. Because that is not my industry. I'm a coach. So in my posts, I coach.

New followers can easily understand the "why".

1. Serve before you sell → I don't shy away from writing a 3,000-character breakdown post. Or coaching people for free in my comments. Or my lives. I give 101%.

This builds trust in my coaching before buying.

1. Write ultra-shareable content → I cannot stress this one enough. If your content isn't reaching NEW 2nd- and 3rd-degree networks, your growth will halt.

This post is an example of "ultra-shareable".

1. Spend HOURS engaging with others. To be frank, I don't even have to post on LinkedIn. I can simply spend my time in the comments and DMs. And it'll still work.

That's how effective manual engagement is. No AI.

Bonus: Never-ever post daily. Simply not necessary.

I really hope you can find a lesson here.

### Annotation

- Demonstrates how consistent positioning and audience service contribute to long-term LinkedIn growth.

- Emphasizes shareable content and manual engagement as key drivers of organic reach.

- Reinforces the importance of building trust through value-first content rather than frequent posting.
